How GeoHeatWhisper Works

Understand the technology behind geo-grid rank tracking and learn how to visualize your Google Maps rankings across every neighborhood.

The 3-Step Process

Step 1: Search

Enter a keyword and location to begin tracking. You can search by:

  • City: "Boston, MA" or "San Francisco, CA"
  • Neighborhood: "Back Bay, Boston" or "Mission District, SF"
  • Zip Code: "02116" or "94110"
  • Address: Any specific street address or intersection

Then specify your target keyword, such as "plumber", "italian restaurant", "dentist near me", or any service your business provides. Select your grid size (1×1 on Free, up to 7×7 on Pro/Agency) and grid spacing to control coverage area.

Step 2: Analyze

GeoHeatWhisper breaks your target area into a geographic grid and checks Google Maps rankings from each grid cell. The result is an interactive heatmap showing:

  • Red (Rank 1–3) — Areas where you rank at the very top
  • Orange (Rank 4–10) — Good visibility, room to improve
  • Yellow (Rank 11–20) — Lower visibility, optimization needed
  • Grey (Unranked) — Not appearing in results from this location

Click any cell to see the exact ranking position, competing businesses, and the top 20 results for that specific location.

Step 3: Act & Track

Use the data to optimize your local SEO strategy:

  • Save to Leads: Save businesses from results with tagging & notes (Pro/Agency)
  • CSV Export: Download raw ranking data for further analysis (Pro/Agency)
  • Automated Reports: Set up weekly or monthly email reports (Pro/Agency)
  • Historical Comparison: Compare snapshots over time to measure progress (Pro/Agency)

What is a Geo-Grid?

A geo-grid is a revolutionary way to visualize local search performance. Traditional rank checkers only show your position from a single point. GeoHeatWhisper divides your target area into a grid of cells and checks rankings from each cell.

Available grid sizes:

  • 1×1 grid (1 point, 1 credit) — Single-point check, available on Free plan
  • 3×3 grid (9 points, 9 credits) — Good for neighborhood-level overview
  • 5×5 grid (25 points, 25 credits) — Detailed area coverage
  • 7×7 grid (49 points, 49 credits) — Maximum detail across a wide area

This reveals the spatial reality of local SEO: your business might rank #1 from downtown but #15 from the suburbs. Understanding this geographic variance is critical for:

  • Identifying underperforming neighborhoods that need targeted optimization
  • Discovering hot zones where you're already dominating
  • Understanding how proximity affects rankings
  • Tracking expansion into new service areas
  • Comparing performance against competitors across the entire market

Pro Tip: Grid Size vs. Grid Spacing

Grid size determines how many points you check (and credits consumed). Grid spacing determines the distance between points. A 5×5 grid with 1-mile spacing covers a 5×5 mile area, while the same grid with 0.5-mile spacing covers a 2.5×2.5 mile area. Start broader, then zoom into areas of interest.

Understanding Credits

Each grid point represents one credit. When you search for "plumber in Boston" with a 5×5 grid, that uses 25 credits from your monthly allocation.

Credit allocation by plan:

  • Free Plan: 10 credits/month — Enough for one 3×3 scan plus a single-point scan
  • Pro Plan ($49/mo): 1,000 credits/month — Up to 20 large (7×7) scans per month
  • Agency Plan ($249/mo): Unlimited credits — No limits for agencies managing multiple clients

Credits reset at the start of each billing period and do not roll over.
